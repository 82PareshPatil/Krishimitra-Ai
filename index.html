Our Work

Problems Solved By Krishimitra AI

  • Problems Before The platform

High Cost of Farming Inputs

Farmers pay inflated prices for fertilizers and equipment due to fragmented purchasing power.

Lack of Expert Guidance

Limited access to modern farming techniques and mentorship reduces crop yield and quality.

Middlemen Exploitation

Farmers lose profits to intermediaries who buy produce cheaply and sell at inflated market rates.

Organic Resource Inaccessibility

Chemical-free fertilizers remain unaffordable or unavailable to small-scale farmers.

Market Price Disparity

A ₹4/kg tomato production cost becomes ₹50/kg for consumers, bypassing fair farmer compensation.

Inefficient Supply Chains

Poor storage and logistics lead to spoilage and dependency on exploitative third parties.

Weather Vulnerability

Unpredictable climate changes destroy crops due to inadequate forecasting tools.

Unorganized Community Efforts

Farmers lack collective bargaining power for bulk purchases or shared resources.
